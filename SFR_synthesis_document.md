## Worked example: φ as algebraic with self-relating f

The golden ratio φ = (1 + √5)/2 ≈ 1.618 satisfies φ² = φ + 1.
- Fractional part of φ: f(φ) = 0.618...
- Fractional part of φ²: f(φ²) = f(φ + 1) = 0.618...

**The fractional parts are equal.** This is a second-order invariance derivable directly from the defining polynomial. The framework reads this as: algebraic irrationals have polynomial self-relations on their fractional parts; transcendentals do not.
