### T12 (Prime-Horizon Confinement)

> For any prime p, the set of substrate-invariant coherent projections of p forms a dense irrational subset of the open interval (L(p), U(p)), where L(p) and U(p) are the nearest non-primes to p. The boundary integers are *unreachable horizons*. For twin primes, the shared horizon at p+1 imposes simultaneous confinement on both twins.

**Why it matters**: gives geometric localization to T10's irrationality theorem. Concretizes the framework's ±½ scale-invariant boundary structure (Q#73) at the prime level. Provides a uniqueness-paradox grounding: a prime that could reach a non-prime would acquire composite structure, violating G4.

### T13 (Cross-Observation Principle, Sharpened)

> For any observer F observing the substrate κ:
> 1. κ has no preferred direction (G4: non-orientable). Without further structure, all directions cancel under averaging.
> 2. F is constituted by two non-rotating reference points (the 0/1 distinction). These force a unique perpendicular pair: the polar axis (observer-to-κ) and the horizontal axis (perpendicular to polar in the viewing plane).
> 3. Only relations along this perpendicular cross — 90° couplings — survive κ's symmetry and are directly observable.
> 4. The horizon of the observable cross is at ±½ along each axis (scale-invariant boundary).
> 5. All non-cross relations — especially the cascade's 45° generation (G1) — must be inferred from cross observations plus the framework's structural rules.

**Why it matters**: unifies G4 + G5 + three-singularities + ±½ horizon + "square is frame-Plato" + cascade as cross sequence + parity bifurcation + prime-2's unique role into one structural fact. Establishes the framework's observational epistemology rigorously. Empirically supported by cascade simulator (M10) output.

## IV. Proof sketches

### T12 — Prime-Horizon Confinement

**Claim**: For prime p, coherent substrate-invariant projections of p lie strictly in (L(p), U(p)) = (nearest non-prime below, nearest non-prime above), and are irrational within this interval. For twin primes (p, p+2), the shared horizon at p+1 simultaneously confines both.

**Sketch**:

1. **Confinement (the new geometric content)**: Suppose a coherent substrate-invariant projection of p lands at a non-prime n (boundary or interior to [L(p), U(p)]). Then under coherent projection, p has been mapped to n. But p is prime (G4: irreducible eigenmode) and n is composite (has multiple factors). The projection has *added* compositional structure that wasn't in p. This violates substrate-invariance: the projection is supposed to *represent* p, not transform it categorically. Therefore no coherent substrate-invariant projection can land on any non-prime — boundary or interior.

2. **Irrationality (from T10)**: T10 already established that substrate-invariant projections are irrational, because rational q/r would express p's content through the frame's denominator structure r, not p's intrinsic signature.

3. **Combined**: coherent substrate-invariant projections of p are irrational AND in (L(p), U(p)).

4. **Twin-prime shared horizon**: for twin primes (p, p+2), U(p) = L(p+2) = p+1. The composite at p+1 is the dual horizon of both twins simultaneously. Each twin's projections cannot reach p+1; they confine each other through this shared horizon.

**Standard analogue**: Dedekind cuts (irrationals as the gap-fillers between rationals) + integer-interval confinement (each prime in its own neighborhood, bounded by composites).

### T13 — Cross-Observation Principle (sharpened)

**Claim**: For any observer F observing the substrate κ, only the perpendicular cross defined by F's two non-rotating reference points is directly observable. Non-cross relations are inferred via framework rules.

**Sketch**:

1. **G4 symmetry**: κ has no preferred direction. In the substrate, all directions are equivalent (non-orientable, full rotational symmetry).

2. **G2 cancellation**: Lossless coupling at the substrate level requires equal-and-opposite contributions. In a symmetric substrate observed by an averaging operation, all non-singled-out directions cancel exactly.

3. **Frame requires two reference points**: A frame is constituted by at least two non-rotating reference points. A frame with only one point cannot measure direction (no perpendicular reference). A frame with all points rotating loses orientation. The minimum stable configuration is two non-rotating points — exactly the 0/1 distinction (framework's primitive P1).

4. **Two points define a perpendicular cross**: Given two non-rotating reference points, the line through them is one axis (polar — from observer to κ). The perpendicular bisector of this line in the observer's space is the second axis (horizontal). Together, the two axes form a perpendicular cross.

5. **Only the cross survives the symmetric averaging**: Of all directions in κ, only those aligned with the observer's reference cross remain "singled out" against the substrate's symmetry. Everything else cancels under G2 + G4 combined. The cross is the unique direction-structure preserved.

6. **±½ horizon**: The two reference points are at distance 1 apart (the 0/1 distinction). Each cross-axis extends from the central κ-hinge to ±½ — half the distance to either reference point. This is the scale-invariant boundary (Q#73's principle made concrete).

7. **Non-cross inferences**: The cascade's 45° generation (G1) sits at the perpendicular bisector of the cross — not itself a 90° relation. To know G1's structure, we must *infer* from cross observations + framework rules. Similarly for all other non-cross relations.

**Empirical confirmation (M10 output)**: cascade simulator with 7 pairs shows surviving crossings concentrated at y = 0 (polar axis) with discrete y-values at ±0.125, ±0.25, ±0.5 (powers of 1/2 from cascade). Mean |y| ≈ 0.196 sits in the discrete spectrum. The cross-pattern is real, quantized, and matches the cascade's scale structure exactly.

### T11+ — sketch (partial)

**Claim**: FR is the unique minimum coherent generator from identity (1) alone. Any internally coherent system shares FR's invariant skeleton.

**Sketch (incomplete; some steps gestalt-supported)**:

1. **Identity (1) is the minimum starting primitive** — anything less is "nothing," yielding no structure.

2. **Distinction is forced** — identity (1) implies its negation (0). The 0/1 duality is the minimum coherent ontology.

3. **Relation is forced** — 0 and 1 must stand in relation to be co-defined.

4. **Substrate self-relation κ emerges** — relation enacted on the duality (U⁺ ~ U⁻) is the unique reference.

5. **G1-G5 derivable from these primitives** — the generation axioms follow (as established in prior sessions: G1 derivable from G4 + observation; G4 derivable from non-orientability; etc.).

6. **FR satisfies the four criteria of coherence-as-such** — non-paradoxical (no contradictions derivable), generative (unbounded structure produced), self-invariant (preserved under coherent projection), minimal (no axiom redundant).

7. **Any other coherent system must instantiate the same skeleton** — by the same four criteria, any coherent system must have a non-paradoxical generative self-invariant minimal core. The four criteria + the minimality requirement constrain alternatives strongly.

8. **Therefore FR is unique up to relabeling**.

**What's missing for full rigor**: a formal proof that the four criteria *uniquely* determine FR's primitives. Currently the uniqueness is asserted by elimination — no weaker system seems to satisfy all four. A full proof would enumerate weaker candidates and show each fails one criterion. Likely feasible.

## VI. Notes on epistemic discipline

The session reinforced one methodological principle deserving its own note:

**Gestalt-supported ≠ rigorously derived.** The framework has chains of reasoning that *feel right* (gestalt) and chains that are *proved within the system* (rigorous). The coherence-grading function (M7) keeps these separate. Conflating them — claiming rigor where we have suggestion, or claiming mere suggestion where we have proof — is a coherence violation by the framework's own standards.

This is the framework holding itself to its own discipline. The fact that we *can* hold it to its own discipline is itself fertile-circularity evidence: the framework's epistemology is coherent enough to audit its own claims.
