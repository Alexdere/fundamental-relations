## I. Tools (FR Machines)

### M1 — CF expansion as substrate-frame projection operator
- **Status**: 🟢 Active
- **What it does**: Decomposes any real number C into (integer head, CF tail). Integer head names which substrate primes are present; tail is bijective coordinate of how they interfere.
- **Anchor in framework**: Four-layer number ontology (rationals = finite CF; algebraic deg 2 = periodic CF; patterned transcendentals = generating rule, non-periodic; structureless transcendentals = no pattern). T10 (Substrate-Invariance ⟺ Irrationality) operationalized via CF.
- **Where used**: π = 3 + tail (Q#57); φ as Gauss-map fixed point; cell-6 cluster discovery; CF safari across 25+ constants.

### M2 — Cascade operator (1+i)/2 and (1/√2)^n harmonic ladder
- **Status**: 🟢 Active
- **What it does**: Generates a √2-spiral of magnitudes (1/√2)^n. Each step ≈ "2 inversions + 1 displacement" per PATHWEAVE bridge. Real-axis projection at level 5 = scaling by −1/8.
- **Anchor in framework**: G1 generates 45° flows; iteration is the cascade. Cascade endpoint L∞ = 4 + 2√2 = 1/((2−√2)/4) is the framework's first triangulated exact identity from two independent recipes.
- **Where used**: Level-5 near-misses (m_n/m_e × (1/√2)^5 ≈ 325); harmonic structure of [3, 23] interval (tentative).

### M3 — Gauss-map orbits (encoder-space probe)
- **Status**: 🟡 Promising but encoder-only
- **What it does**: Iterates the Gauss map T(x) = {1/x} on a constant's fractional part, tracing its orbit through (0, 1). Reveals fixed points (φ → 0.618 forever), 1-cycles (√2 → 0.414), and rest-value attractors where multiple constants synchronize.
- **Anchor in framework**: Encoder-space tool. **Not** framework-internal — flagged as such in Q#13–14 (afternoon 2026-05-16). Its results are hypotheses, not theorems.
- **Where used**: Rest-value attractor windows at 0.22, 0.52, 0.08, 0.06. φ as primary basin. ζ(3) as most cosmopolitan constant.

### M4 — Prime decomposition of mass ratios
- **Status**: 🟢 Active
- **What it does**: Factors physical mass ratios into prime-power signatures: m_p/m_e ≈ 1836 = 2²·3³·17; m_n/m_e signatures; particle decompositions.
- **Anchor in framework**: G4 (primes as substrate's orthogonal eigenmodes). Composites as interference patterns. Ω = total prime-power count = substrate-fundamentality measure.

### M5 — PSLQ integer-relation finder
- **Status**: 🟡 Use with caveat

- **Anchor in framework**: Useful for *frame-level* (encoder-space) independence checks. **Caveat**: PSLQ-independence is NOT substrate-level independence. Different recipes can produce PSLQ-independent decimals while sharing substrate generators.
